VMotionX

Type a prompt → 3D motion for characters and robots → export for your DCC or rig. Fully offline.

A self-contained desktop studio that turns a plain-text prompt into 3D articulated motion, then lets you author it like any other clip: pose joints, capture and interpolate keyframes, route the subject through an environment with waypoints and foot-locks, extend a motion past its single-shot length, and export clean data to Blender, Unreal, Unity, or a robotics pipeline. It began as a character-animation tool, but because it authors joint trajectories on an articulated rig, the same workflow carries over to humanoid robots and other articulated systems. The generation engine runs entirely on-device; its internals are proprietary and still evolving.

VClaw

A personal AI that operates your own machines. Local-first, with no third party in the loop.

One assistant wired into a fleet of your devices (desktop, laptops, home server) that can actually do things: open apps, move files, run commands, grab a screenshot, reboot a box, all from your phone, from anywhere. It runs on your own infrastructure, so no message, file, or command is routed through someone else’s servers, and no outage but your own can take it offline.
